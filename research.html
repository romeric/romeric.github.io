Novel computational frameworks for coupled electro-magneto-mechanics:

I carry out research on developing novel computational frameworks for coupled electro-magneto-mechanical problems. The point of departure for most of these problems is, an energy functional for the coupled system whose consistent linearisation yield a variational principle. I focus mainly on linearised electromechanical systems with nonlinear electric polarisation characteristics undergoing small (geometrically linear) deformations which are still variationally consistent i.e. they emanate from an underlying energy principle. To achieve this, starting from an objective and multi-variable convex nonlinear electroelastic energy is always desirable. The energy is then perturbed in the vicinity of stress free (undeformed) configuration, in order to particularise its truely nonlinear variational principle into a linearised form while maintaining all the favourable features from the nonlinear constitutive model. For a short illustration, consider the potential energy of a simple electroelastic system

\Pi(\boldsymbol{x},\boldsymbol{D}) = \int_\Omega \Psi(\boldsymbol{F},\boldsymbol{H},J,\boldsymbol{D}) dV,

where $\Psi$ is a polyconvex internal energy for the coupled electroelastic system, $\boldsymbol{x}$ is the position vector of the material point, $\boldsymbol{F}$ is the deformation gradient tensor, $\boldsymbol{H}$ is the cofactor of the deformation gradient tensor, $J$ is the determinant of the deformation gradient tensor, $\boldsymbol{D}$ is the electric displacement vector and $f(J)$ is typically a convex function in $J$, such as

\Psi(\boldsymbol{F},\boldsymbol{H},J,\boldsymbol{D}) = \alpha\boldsymbol{F}:\boldsymbol{F} + \beta\boldsymbol{H}:\boldsymbol{H} + f(J) + \frac{1}{2\mu}\boldsymbol{D}:\boldsymbol{D},

where $\alpha$, $\beta$ and $\mu$ are material constants with positive values. The mechanically linearised potential energy after applying a perturbation in the form of Taylor series expansion can now be written as

\Pi_{\text{lin}}(\boldsymbol{x},\boldsymbol{D}) = \int_\Omega \bigg(\Psi(\boldsymbol{F},\boldsymbol{H},J,\boldsymbol{D}) + D\Psi(\boldsymbol{F},\boldsymbol{H},J,\boldsymbol{D})[\delta\boldsymbol{u}] + \frac{1}{2}D^2\Psi(\boldsymbol{F},\boldsymbol{H},J,\boldsymbol{D})[\delta\boldsymbol{u};\Delta\boldsymbol{u}] \bigg) dV,

$\Pi_{\text{lin}}$ is a new energy functional which is geometrically linearised but electrostatically still nonlinear. Further linearisation of this energy leads an electromechanical variational formulation which can be solved using a staggered scheme. The variational form is then discretised using suitable finite element spaces. In this context high order finite elements which can capture the exact geometry and result in higher accuracy are applied. For mathematical and implemenation details the reader can refer to the papers by Gil and Ortigosa and Poya et. al.

High Order Curvilinear Mesh Generation:

A core part of my research work focuses on implementing novel algorithms for generating structured and unstructured meshes for NURBS conforming high order finite element analysis. In particular, I work on various solid mechanics based analogies for a posteriori mesh generation.

Further details about this project can be found in the PostMesh official GitHub repository. For mathematical and computational details the reader can refer to the following research papers.

  • R. Poya, R. Sevilla, A. J. Gil, A unified approach for a posteriori high-order curved mesh generation using solid mechanics, Computational Mechanics, Vol 58, Issue 3, pp 457-490, (2016)
  • R. Poya, R. Sevilla, A. J. Gil, A consistently linearised solid mechanics based mesh deformation technique for high order curved elements, European Congress on Computational Methods in Applied Sciences and Engineering (ECCOMAS), Crete Island, Greece, 5-10 June 2016

High Performance Data Parallel Approaches to Solving Multi-Physics Problems:

Recently, I have started implementing a data parallel tensor contraction framework based on C++11 (and beyond) with some fundamentally novel metaproramming features. In particular, the framework combines powerful SIMD implementations of BLAS type routines for high order tensors with a domain-aware expression template engine which performs operation minimisation at compile time.

Novel computational frameworks for coupled electro-magneto-mechanics:

I carry out research on developing novel computational frameworks for coupled electro-magneto-mechanical problems. The point of departure for most of these problems is, an energy functional for the coupled system whose consistent linearisation yield a variational principle. I focus mainly on linearised electromechanical systems with nonlinear electric polarisation characteristics undergoing small (geometrically linear) deformations which can still emanate from a variatinally consistent formulations. To this end, starting from an objective, coercive and polyconvex nonlinear electroelastic energy is always desirable. The energy is then perturbed in the vicinity of stress free (undeformed) configuration, in order to transform a truely nonlinear variational principle into its linearised form while maintaining all the favourable features from the nonlinear constitutive model. For instance, consider a the potential energy of a simple polyconvex electroelastic system

\Pi(\vec{x}) = \int_\Omega \Bigg(\boldsymbol{F}:\boldsymbol{F} \bigg) dV

The variational form is then discretised using suitable finite element spaces. In this context high order finite elements which can capture the exact geometry and result in higher accuracy are applied.

This work is mainly focused towards numerical simulations of sensors, energy harversters and soft tissues such as artifical muscles. Further details about this project can be found in the soon-to-be-open-source florence project.

High Order Curvilinear Mesh Generation:

A core part of my research work focuses on implementing novel algorithms for generating structured and unstructured meshes for high order finite element analysis. In particular, I work on various solid mechanics based analogies for a posteriori mesh generation.

Further details about this project can be found in the PostMesh official GitHub repository
